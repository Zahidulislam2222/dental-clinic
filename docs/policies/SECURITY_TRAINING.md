# Security Awareness Training Policy

**Everyday Dental Surgery & Implant Center**
**SOC 2 TSC: CC1 (Control Environment)**
**HIPAA: 164.308(a)(5)**
**Version:** 1.0 | **Effective:** 2026-04-02 | **Review:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

Ensure all workforce members understand their responsibilities for protecting patient data and clinic systems.

## 2. Training Requirements

### 2.1 New Hire Onboarding (Within 30 days)

- [ ] HIPAA Privacy Rule overview
- [ ] HIPAA Security Rule overview
- [ ] Clinic information security policy review
- [ ] Access control policy and role-specific permissions
- [ ] Password policy and MFA setup
- [ ] PHI handling — what constitutes PHI, minimum necessary standard
- [ ] Incident reporting procedures
- [ ] Email security — no PHI in email, phishing awareness
- [ ] Physical security — screen locks, visitor management
- [ ] Sign acknowledgment form

### 2.2 Annual Refresher Training

- HIPAA updates and regulatory changes
- Review of past year's security incidents and lessons learned
- Phishing simulation results and remediation
- Updated policies and procedures
- Role-specific training updates

### 2.3 Role-Specific Training

| Role | Additional Training |
|------|-------------------|
| Admin | User management, audit log review, incident response, BAA management |
| Doctor | PHI access procedures, patient portal demo, FHIR data standards |
| Receptionist | Non-PHI access boundaries, appointment management, front desk security |
| IT | System administration, patch management, backup verification, Edge Function deployment |

## 3. Phishing Awareness

- Quarterly simulated phishing emails
- Immediate remedial training for staff who fail simulations
- Report suspicious emails to Security Officer

## 4. Training Delivery

- In-person briefings for onboarding
- Annual training via documented slide deck / video
- Policy acknowledgments signed and stored

## 5. Training Records

- All training completion records stored for 6 years
- Records include: trainee name, date, topics covered, acknowledgment signature
- Training records are part of SOC 2 audit evidence

## 6. Non-Compliance

Failure to complete required training within specified timeframes may result in:
1. First occurrence: Written warning
2. Second occurrence: Access privileges suspended until training complete
3. Third occurrence: Disciplinary action
