### 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** "PHI" means Protected Health Information as defined in 45 CFR 160.103.
**1.2** "ePHI" means Electronic Protected Health Information as defined in 45 CFR 160.103.
**1.3** "Security Incident" means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations.
**1.4** "Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.
**1.5** "HIPAA Rules" means the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164.

---

### 2. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**2.1 Permitted Uses.** Business Associate may use or disclose PHI solely for the purpose of performing services described in the underlying service agreement, and as required by law.

**2.2 Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI, consistent with the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C).

**2.3 Reporting.** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity:
- Any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for in this Agreement, within **24 hours** of discovery.
- Any Security Incident of which Business Associate becomes aware, within **72 hours** of discovery.
- Any Breach of Unsecured PHI, within **24 hours** of discovery.

**2.4 Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors who create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate agree in writing to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate under this Agreement.

**2.5 Access.** Business Associate shall make PHI available to Covered Entity or the Individual as required under 45 CFR 164.524 (Right of Access) within **15 business days** of a request.

**2.6 Amendment.** Business Associate shall make PHI available for amendment and incorporate amendments as directed by Covered Entity, per 45 CFR 164.526.

**2.7 Accounting.** Business Associate shall make available the information required for Covered Entity to provide an accounting of disclosures per 45 CFR 164.528.

**2.8 HHS Access.** Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of HHS for determining compliance.

**2.9 Minimum Necessary.** Business Associate shall limit its use, disclosure, or request of PHI to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose, per 45 CFR 164.502(b).

---

### 3. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

**3.1** Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with any changes in or revocation of authorization by Individuals to use or disclose their PHI, to the extent such changes affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

**3.2** Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any restrictions on use or disclosure of PHI agreed to per 45 CFR 164.522.

---

### 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1 Term.** This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

**4.2 Termination for Cause.** Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement if Business Associate has violated a material term of this Agreement. Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate **30 days** written notice and opportunity to cure.

**4.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity. If return or destruction is not feasible, protections of this Agreement extend to retained PHI.
- Certify in writing that all PHI has been returned or destroyed, or explain why return/destruction is not feasible.

---

### 5. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

Business Associate represents and warrants that it maintains, at minimum:

- [ ] Encryption of ePHI at rest (AES-256 or equivalent)
- [ ] Encryption of ePHI in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- [ ] Access controls with unique user identification
- [ ] Audit logging of all PHI access
- [ ] Regular security assessments (at least annually)
- [ ] Workforce security training
- [ ] Disaster recovery and data backup procedures
- [ ] Incident response plan

---

### 6. INDEMNIFICATION

Business Associate shall indemnify and hold harmless Covered Entity from any claims, losses, or damages arising from Business Associate's violation of this Agreement or applicable HIPAA Rules.

---

### 7. MISCELLANEOUS

**7.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by applicable federal HIPAA regulations.
**7.2 Amendment.** This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.
**7.3 Survival.** The obligations of Business Associate under Sections 2 and 4.3 survive termination.
**7.4 Annual Review.** Both parties agree to review this Agreement annually and update as necessary.
